How we choose our picks

We don’t run our own typing tests or measure decibel levels in a lab. Instead, we read hundreds of detailed owner reviews on forums, retailers, and dedicated mechanical keyboard communities, looking for patterns in what holds up over months of daily use and what tends to break. We also cross‑reference published reviews from reputable tech outlets and keyboard specialists who do their own testing, and we track long‑term feedback threads where people report on hot‑swap socket durability, Bluetooth dropout rates, and switch spring fatigue. Our picks prioritize consistency and real‑world reliability over flashy specs. A keyboard that sounds great in a YouTube video but has a poor stabiliser rattle or a flimsy USB port after six months won’t make our list. We also weigh the ease of modification (if you’re into that) and the quality of included accessories such as keycaps and cables. The three boards featured here have been widely praised for their build, and their trade‑offs are clearly noted, so you know exactly what you’re getting before you buy.
