How we choose our picks

We don’t run a testing lab or chase launch-day hype. Instead, we rely on a combination of long-term owner reports from forums like r/MechanicalKeyboards, expert reviews from trusted publications, and our own careful reading of what actually fails, or doesn’t. A keyboard earns a spot here after months of consistent praise and verifiable durability, not a one-week honeymoon. Build quality, switch options, hot-swap compatibility, software support, and real-world customer support experiences all factor into our ratings. We also track return rates and common complaints. If a board has a known issue with double-typing or a flimsy USB port three months in, it doesn’t make the list, no matter how good it feels out of the box.
