Frequently asked questions

What switch is best for gaming?

Linear switches (Cherry MX Red, Speed Silver, or equivalents) are most common for gaming, smooth travel with no tactile bump means faster actuation and less finger fatigue. That said, many competitive gamers use tactile switches with no performance disadvantage.

Does polling rate matter for gaming?

1,000 Hz (1ms polling) is standard and sufficient for competitive gaming. 4,000-8,000 Hz polling keyboards exist but the real-world benefit is negligible for most games. Polling rate matters less than actual switch actuation speed and consistency.

What is anti-ghosting and N-key rollover?

Ghosting is when pressing multiple keys simultaneously causes some to not register. Anti-ghosting prevents this for defined key combinations. N-key rollover (NKRO) means every key registers independently no matter how many you press at once, the gold standard for gaming.

Should I get a gaming keyboard with a numpad?

Most PC gamers prefer tenkeyless, dropping the numpad lets you position the mouse closer to the keyboard, reducing the arm extension that causes fatigue in long gaming sessions. Numpad-free layouts are the default for competitive play.

Are wireless mechanical keyboards reliable for gaming?

Modern wireless keyboards with 2.4GHz USB dongles (not Bluetooth) are effectively lag-free for gaming, the latency difference from wired is imperceptible. Bluetooth introduces slightly more latency and is less reliable under wireless interference; for competitive play, use the 2.4GHz dongle connection.
